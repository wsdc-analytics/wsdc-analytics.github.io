WSDC Rules Evolution: 24 Years of Development

Interactive timeline of WSDC rules changes from 2002 to 2026

Over 24 years, WSDC rules have evolved from a simple range-based system to a complex threshold model with detailed requirements. This timeline shows the key changes that shaped the modern West Coast Swing competition system.

Timeline of Changes

Click on a year to see details of changes. The last four years are expanded by default.

2026
  • Additional fee charged to competitors (Competitor Surcharge) increased to $2 per competition entry (as of January 1, 2026)
  • Added new section to rules: WSDC Judges Certification Program, which will launch on January 1, 2027
  • Updated definition of Tier 5 (competition level): now events with 80-129 competitors (previously 40-79)
  • Clarified rules for petitions (requests to change division), secondary roles (when dancer competes in different role), and contest structure
Latest version of rules at the time of publication. The judges certification program shows movement toward professionalization of judging.

2025
  • New annual membership fee (Membership Fee): $200 per official WSDC event (Registry Event)
  • Additional fee charged to competitors (Competitor Surcharge): $1 per entry, with planned increase to $2 as of January 1, 2026
  • Trial Events (new events seeking official status) must now use certified chief judge and award points to participants
  • Minimum number of participants for official events: 120 entries (80 for developing regions)
  • Updated rules for secondary role (when dancer competes in role different from primary)
  • New "Action Plan" process for cases of rule violations by event organizers
Significant changes in financial structure and event requirements. Trial Events are now fully integrated into the points system.
Download original document (PDF)
2024
  • New thresholds for Champions division access: allowed at 1 Champion point or 150 All Star points; required at 10 Champion points or 225 All Star points
  • Petition to move up division (Petition up): now possible only through WSDC Chief Judges Committee, submission 15 days before event with 2 videos attached
  • Formally introduced callback scoring standard (for callbacks in competitions): Yes=10 points, Alt1=4.5, Alt2=4.3, Alt3=4.2, No=0
  • Updated time and distance rule (Time/Distance Rule): now applies globally - 200 miles or 5 weekend days between events
  • Strengthened requirements for WSDC membership, penalties for violations, and prevention of conflicts of interest
Unprecedented update activity (4 versions per year) shows active system development and rapid response to community needs.
Download version 2024.2B (PDF)
2023
  • Complete revision of rules structure for event organizers (Event Rules)
  • Clarified venue requirements for competitions: minimum 150 sq.m of dance floor space, requirements for layout and participant services
  • Confirmed thresholds for All-Star division: 150 points for allowed access, 225 points for required
  • Strengthened restrictions for secondary role: dancer can compete in different role only one level below primary division
  • Detailed procedures for developing regions: alternative option - minimum 80 participants + 100 attendees instead of standard 120 participants
Significant document expansion reflects accumulated experience after COVID and the need to detail standards for a growing community.
Download version 2023.1D (PDF)
2020
  • Access to Champions division: All Star dancers with 150 or more points must be admitted to Champion Jack & Jill
  • 2021 Addendum: allowed to compete in both roles (leader and follower) in one division with separate point tracking for each role
  • 2021 Addendum: freeze of "3-year window" rule for Advanced and All Star divisions due to COVID-19 pandemic
  • 2021 Addendum: ban on virtual judging of competitions
  • 2021 Addendum: temporary reduction of minimum participants to 60 for 2021 to support events during pandemic
  • Added flexibility in venue selection: special requirements for non-standard locations (special venue)
Critical change that allowed the industry to survive during the pandemic. Rapid adaptation through addendum shows WSDC flexibility.
Download version 2020.1B with addendum (PDF)
2019
  • Mandatory number of rounds: as of July 1, 2019, number of rounds in competition must correspond to event level (Tier)
  • Consolidation of system with 6 event levels (6-tier system) based on number of participants
  • Clarifications on role changes: officially allowed to change role (leader/follower) only within established rules
  • Established requirements for WSDC competitor identification number (Competitor ID) for tracking results
Formalization of competition structure requirements and consolidation of rules for roles and competitor identification.
Download version 2019.1A (PDF)
2018
  • Split single document into two specialized ones: "Points Registry Rules" for dancers and "Registry Event Rules" for event organizers
  • Introduction of system with 6 event levels (6-tier): from Tier 1 (5-10 participants) to Tier 6 (130+ participants)
  • Advancement chart: established "allowed/required" logic for each division (Novice 16/30, Intermediate 30/45, Advanced 45/60, All Star 150/225 points)
  • Formalized responsibilities of event director (Event Director) and competitor
  • Detailed petition rules: maximum division change is ±1 level
  • Clarified role qualification: distinction between primary and secondary role of dancer
Radical change in documentation structure. Separation of responsibilities: rules for dancers ≠ rules for organizers. Each group received a specialized document, simplifying understanding of requirements.
Download version 2018.1A (PDF)
2015
  • Transition from range system to threshold model: Novice less than 15 points, Intermediate from 15 Novice points and less than 30 Intermediate points, Advanced from 30 Intermediate points
  • All Star division: requires minimum 45 Advanced points earned over last 3 years
  • Introduced unified identification number (ID) system for tracking dancer roles
  • Event director (Event Director) must report results to WSDC within 10 days after event
  • Petition process: maximum division change is ±1 level, violations result in no points awarded
  • Leader and follower points tracked separately for each role, but summed when determining advancement to next division
Fundamental transition from rigid ranges to threshold system, giving dancers more flexibility in choosing timing for division transitions.
Download version 2015 (PDF)
2011
  • New principle for determining event level (Tier): now level determined separately for leaders and separately for followers
  • One competition can have two different point allocation tables - one for leaders, another for followers
  • Minimum requirement: at least 5 leaders and 5 followers, otherwise event not counted for point allocation
Recognition of inequality in number of leaders and followers in competitions. This allowed more fair point allocation based on actual number of participants in each role.
Download version 2011 (PDF)
2009
  • Strict requirements for transitions: from Novice to Intermediate only with 20 points; from Intermediate to Advanced requires 25 points
  • Point allocation by event levels (Tier): for Tier 1 - 5/4/3/2/1 points for placements; for Tier 3 - 15/12/10/8/6 points
  • Restrictions: one participant can compete in only one Jack & Jill division, one identification number (ID) used for both roles
Establishment of clear minimum requirements for division transitions. Increased rewards for large contests (Tier3) incentivized participation in major events.
Download version 2009 (PDF)
2007
  • First introduction of event level system (Tier 1-3), depending on number of couples participating in competition
  • Point allocation by levels: Tier 1 - 8/6/4/2/1 points, Tier 2 - 10/8/6/4/2 points, Tier 3 - 12/10/8/6/4 points for placements
  • All Star division: first mentioned as separate tracked division (recommended minimum 40 Advanced points for access)
  • Advancement rules: in Novice can stay until 20 points + first place; in Intermediate until 25 points + first place
  • Term "mass number of events" appears to describe active competitors
Introduction of Tier system became foundation for modern points structure. Emergence of All-Star as separate division created intermediate level between Advanced and Champions.
Download version 2007 (PDF)
2004
  • Added FAQ section with detailed scenarios: competitions with finals only (finals-only), combined divisions
  • Restrictions: if division has fewer than 5 couples, points not awarded
  • Recommendations for dancers: stay in division until earning 20 points and getting first place
  • Maintained point range system for divisions: Beginner/Newcomer (1-10), Novice (1-19), Intermediate (20-29), Advanced (30+)
Clarifications of existing system without radical changes. "20 points" recommendation became standard for following years.
Download version 2004 (PDF)
2002
  • Established basic point range system for divisions: Beginner/Newcomer (1-10), Novice (1-19), Intermediate (20-29), Advanced (30+)
  • Simple point allocation system: fixed scale 10/6/4/3/2/1 for places 1-6, all finalists receive 1 point
  • Champions division: created only at event organizer's discretion, not mandatory
  • Points Registry maintained by WSDC since 1993 to track competitor results
  • Recommendation for dancers: stay in division until getting first place before moving to next
First available WSDC rules document. Establishes basic division structure and points system. Points Registry began in 1993, meaning 9 years of history before this document.
Download version 2002 (PDF)
